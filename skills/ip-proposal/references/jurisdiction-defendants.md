# Jurisdiction And Defendants

## Jurisdiction Sequence

Analyze courts in this order:

1. Defendant domicile:
   - Company registered address, individual domicile, shop operator place, producer address.
2. Infringing act place:
   - Manufacturing, warehousing, shipment, sale, upload, server operation, project use, store operation, platform operation.
3. Infringing result place:
   - Notarized purchase receiving place, plaintiff impact place, software use place, content access place. Treat network result-place jurisdiction cautiously and verify local practice.
4. Platform connection:
   - Platform operator domicile if platform is a defendant or its conduct is a jurisdiction connector; otherwise usually use platform for evidence production, not as a defendant.
5. Centralized IP jurisdiction and level:
   - Verify current local rules, court level, amount threshold, specialized IP court/tribunal, patent technical case rules.

## Candidate Court Output

For each candidate court, state:

- Court name.
- Connection fact.
- Evidence proving the connection.
- Advantages.
- Jurisdiction objection risk.
- Missing jurisdiction evidence.

## Defendant Selection

Use search results to expand or narrow defendants. A named company is only the starting point; packaging labels, shop licenses, invoice issuers, payees, logistics senders, account holders, shareholders, and related stores may be better defendants for damages, evidence production, or jurisdiction.

### Trademark

Core defendants:

- Producer/source manufacturer.
- Seller/store operator.
- Brand operator or packaging controller.
- Importer/exporter if applicable.

Add when supported:

- Platform only if notice-and-failure, self-operation, active recommendation, or deep participation exists.
- Shareholder/actual controller for one-person company, mixed assets, personal collection, joint operation.

### Unfair Competition

Core defendants depend on conduct:

- Trade dress/name passing off: brand operator, producer, seller, packaging/invoice/shipper.
- Internet/data/traffic: product operator, technical developer, account holder, platform or distribution channel if deeply involved.
- Trade secret: recipient/user of secret, former employee, new employer, affiliates using the information.

Include local seller when it is needed for jurisdiction and supply chain proof, but do not let a small seller define the whole damages theory.

### Patent

Primary target is the source:

- Manufacturer.
- OEM/commissioning party.
- Product brand/operator.
- Project user or engineering contractor for method/device use.
- Sales company/distributor for sale and jurisdiction.

Avoid a retail-only case when a source defendant can be found.

### Copyright

Core defendants:

- Uploader/publisher/seller/software user.
- Account/operator receiving income.
- Platform only when it failed after valid notice, actively recommended, edited, distributed, shared revenue, or self-operated.
- Content purchaser/issuer/licensee if it exceeded authorization.

## Joint Liability Signals

Use these facts to plead joint liability:

- Same legal representative, shareholder, phone, address, employees, warehouse, account.
- Same product packaging, customer service, payment account, invoice issuer.
- One company manufactures, another sells, another receives money.
- One-person company cannot prove asset independence.
- Controller shifts links/accounts after complaints.

## Exclusion Discipline

Explain why a party is not included when relevant:

- Platform as evidence holder only.
- Small retailer as jurisdiction anchor only.
- Individual shareholder reserved pending asset-mixing proof.
- Manufacturer unknown pending notarized purchase/logistics/invoice.
