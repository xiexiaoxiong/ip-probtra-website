# Workflow

## Fact Hierarchy

Use four fact levels:

- `已核验事实`: official registry, court document, notarized evidence, original document, platform page actually viewed, or user-provided source file.
- `强推定事实`: multiple public sources are consistent but not notarized or official.
- `待核验事实`: user statement, inaccessible link, screenshot not inspected, single non-official clue.
- `法律判断`: route recommendation, damages feasibility, jurisdiction assessment, defense prediction.

## Investigation Steps

1. Identify the object:
   - Commodity, packaging, software, image, video, game, data, trade secret, service, project, store, App, platform account.
2. Identify rights first:
   - Before selecting a cause of action, search the right holder's and accused side's trademarks, patents/designs, and copyright/source materials.
   - Use `rights-search-methods.md` for source priority and fallback methods.
   - Include accused-side rights because own trademarks, later design patents, or claimed copyright can be defenses or bad-faith clues.
3. Preserve the infringement:
   - Product/content page, item ID, store/account, title, images, details, brand field, price, sales/reviews/downloads/plays, timestamps.
   - For physical goods, always capture and compare the plaintiff product image and the accused product image before route selection. Record visual features: container shape, color system, layout grid, logo position, typography, product-name placement, icons/seals, claims, model/scene imagery, and overall impression.
   - If public browser cannot access dynamic platform details, follow `access-priority.md` before requiring App/miniprogram notarization.
4. Identify actors:
   - Seller, producer, brand operator, uploader, account operator, software user, platform operator, payee, invoice issuer, logistics sender, shareholder/actual controller.
5. Identify money:
   - Price, sales, GMV, contract amount, project amount, ads, membership, software license price, copyright license price, patent contribution, profit rate.
6. Identify bad faith:
   - Prior cases, warnings, platform complaints, repeated links, re-upload/re-listing, same controller, counterfeit, low price, refusal to provide data.
7. Expand the clues:
   - Turn every seed signal into search terms: brand names, product names, item IDs, shop names, company names, addresses, package text, barcodes, SKU specs, legal representatives, phone numbers, and platform account names.
   - Search across likely sales channels and public web before deciding the route or amount.
   - Record failed/inaccessible searches; they define the notarization and court investigation plan.

## Public Investigation Targets

- Right holder official site, annual reports, flagship stores, awards, prior judgments.
- Right holder and accused-side trademark, patent/design, and copyright/source-file evidence.
- Whole-web and platform search results for the target product, shop, item ID, company, barcode, and package text.
- E-commerce and content-commerce channels: Pinduoduo, Taobao/Tmall, JD, Douyin, Kuaishou, 1688, Xiaohongshu, WeChat, Baidu/Bing indexed pages, and industry/wholesale sites when relevant.
- CNIPA trademark/patent status and right holder.
- Copyright registration/source publication where available.
- National Enterprise Credit Information Publicity System for companies; third-party sites only as clues.
- Platform IP protection rules and page data.
- Court jurisdiction rules and local IP centralized jurisdiction.

## Do Not Skip

- Link-level evidence and subject-level evidence must be connected. A beautiful infringement screenshot is weak if it does not prove who sold, produced, shipped, invoiced, or collected money.
- Do not stop at "page blocked" if browser rendering, user-assisted login, or App-side notarization is a practical next step. If the page reaches a normal platform login/risk-control screen, first ask the user to complete login or verification in the visible browser/App, then continue the assessment.
- Damages evidence must be its own package, not an appendix to infringement evidence.
- Sales and scale must be actively investigated, not inferred only from the user's stated infringing link.
- Backup route evidence must be listed even if not the primary route.
