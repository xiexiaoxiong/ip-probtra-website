## Scoring Matrix

Score each route 0-2 for each dimension. Total 12.

Do not score from the user's first clue alone when a live target can be investigated. Treat early scores as provisional until search results establish the right status, actual page use, sales scale, actors, and procedural risks.

| Dimension | Trademark | Unfair Competition | Patent | Copyright |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Right certainty | Registered mark and category coverage | Clear competitive interest and influence | Valid/stable patent | Source/registration/license chain |
| Infringement visibility | Mark/logo/brand field use | Trade dress, name, data, traffic, secret, platform behavior | Technical/appearance comparison possible | Protected expression copied |
| Money calculability | Sales, price, profit, brand contribution | Transaction, traffic, project, substitute cost | Contract, sales, profit, contribution rate | License fee, plays/downloads, revenue |
| Bad faith | Counterfeit, repeat, post-complaint | Passing off, circumvention, leakage, rule breaking | Knowing patent, source manufacturing | Bulk copying, notice ignored |
| Defendant utility | Producer/seller/store/platform account | Organizer/platform/source/controller | Manufacturer/project user | Uploader/platform/software user |
| Procedural risk | Category/likeness/authorization | Vague object/general clause | Invalidity/non-infringement/expert proof | Ownership/originality/safe harbor |

Interpretation:

- 9-12: primary path.
- 6-8: backup or parallel path.
- 3-5: evidence development path.
- 0-2: do not plead unless new facts appear.

## Route Rules

### Trademark Primary

Choose trademark when the page/product uses the right holder's registered mark or a confusingly similar sign in title, brand field, logo, packaging, store name, customer service, advertising, or goods/services source indication.

Backup routes:

- Unfair competition if packaging, trade dress, enterprise name, false publicity, or passing off exists.
- Copyright if product/detail page copies images, videos, or designs.

### Unfair Competition Primary

Choose unfair competition when:

- Defendant uses its own brand but copies packaging/trade dress/product name.
- Defendant avoids the exact plaintiff mark but preserves the plaintiff's distinctive visual system, such as container shape, color block, logo/product-name placement, typography hierarchy, certification/seal icons, model/scene images, and overall shelf impression.
- Enterprise name, store decoration, product style, or platform presentation creates confusion.
- Data scraping, traffic hijacking, malicious compatibility, commercial disparagement, or trade secret facts dominate.
- Registered rights do not cover the main wrong, but the conduct violates business ethics.

Example: a product using its own brand but copying the right holder's distinctive packaging should normally be analyzed as trade dress unfair competition first, not trademark first.

### Patent Primary

Choose patent when:

- A valid patent exists and the accused product/method/design can be compared.
- Manufacturing source, project use, contract amount, or high-value product evidence exists.
- The right is stable enough to survive invalidity risk.

Do not choose patent as primary if there is no patent number/status, no claim/design comparison, or the accused product cannot be obtained.

### Copyright Primary

Choose copyright when:

- Images, videos, text, software, game assets, music, font, audiovisual works, or other protected expression are copied or distributed.
- Ownership chain and license market are available.
- There is bulk copying, commercial use, platform-scale dissemination, software use, or high license price.

Do not overvalue single low-use images or short text without commercial scale.

## Tie-Breakers

When several paths score closely, choose the path with:

1. The clearest liability proof.
2. The best damages evidence.
3. The lowest procedural risk.
4. The strongest defendant/source connection.
5. The most favorable jurisdiction option.

Use parallel claims when harms overlap, but state whether damages are the same loss or separately calculable.
